Two hours and ten minutes.

You let yourself hold that number for a moment without immediately stress-testing it, which is actually harder than it sounds. Eight years had taught you that good news generally came with a great deal of fine print.

Four months ago, the portal mages had sealed the last entrance to the surface. There were no gates or tunnels; just a mile of solid rock between the city of Solace and the horrors above. Solace was the last coalition of surviving nations, built into the earth's hollow because there was nowhere else to go but down. Legions of geomancers carved through the rock, artificer mages laid the streets and buildings, and the most powerful illusionists created a false sky on the ceiling so the survivors could look up at something other than stone in despair.

Everyone despaired, regardless. How could they not?

*page_break But it mostly worked, for all of four months.

For four months, as far as anyone knows, there has not been a single living human being on the surface. The monsters had done what monsters do when left entirely to their own devices: autonomously summoned more of their kind, fought and consumed each other to grow more powerful and stranger, and evolved in directions no one could have anticipated. They got smarter, and they eventually began looking for wherever humanity had tried to hide.

It didn't take long for them to start looking down.

But in two hours and ten minutes, none of it will matter anymore. You find, carefully, that you almost believe it.
