*label opening

The world ended on a Tuesday.

It didn't happen all at once. It ended in layers, dying piece by piece: first the small things, then the large ones, and finally the core holding it all together.

The System arrived eight years ago. A notification appeared in every human mind simultaneously, translated into the precise dialect each person understood best.

*system
System Initializing...
Initialization Complete. 
Congratulations! Your world has been inducted to the Multiverse.
*end_system

Nobody laughed. Some people cried. Most just stood very still, reading the floating words and trying to remember how to breathe.

The monsters came three days later.

Eight years of blood and grinding. Eight years of dungeons, raids, and watching the people you loved become grayed-out names on a party list, blinking once before vanishing forever.

You survived. Improbably, infuriatingly, you are still here.

And now you stand on the ramparts of Castle Voss—the last fortress of the final three humans on Earth—watching the horizon burn.

The notification hangs in your vision, a jagged red warning. You dismiss it. It reappears. You dismiss it again. It returns a third time, blinking fast, betraying the System's own panic.

*add_item "Last Blade of Democles"

"Man."

Kai drops down beside you, kicking his heels over the hundred-foot drop with casual indifference. He runs his fingers through his hair. It is longer now, shot through with gray he didn't have two years ago. "Just when you think things can't get any worse."

He laughs. That is Kai's defining trait: laughing at the wrong moments. At funerals. Mid-raid. At the apocalypse. It used to annoy you. Now you consider it an act of profound bravery.

On your other side, Sable remains silent. She hasn't spoken in three days—not since the Siege of the Eastern Wall, not since she returned alone from a four-person scouting mission. She stands with her arms folded, eyes locked on the horizon. Her posture broadcasts a quiet, irreversible decision.

The three of you. The last three. A sky bleeding a necrotic red.
