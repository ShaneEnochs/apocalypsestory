*title The End of the World

*label opening

The air in the bunker city of *Solace* is stale, smelling of cold stone and death. 

When it became clear that the surface was lost, the last standing nations pooled their remaining resources for the Deep-Reach Initiative. Armies of geomancers sheared the earth's mantle to carve these hollows. Architecture mages raised the buildings and created roadways between them. Druids and nature mages wove life into the dark, growing sunless forests and filling reservoirs from deep-earth streams. Illusionists cast spells upon the rock ceiling to simulate a sky and a sun that stayed at high noon, though it faded to a glow like that of the moon during night hours. Finally, the portal mages brought the survivors down, sealing the way behind them. There were no gates or tunnels leading down here; just a mile of solid rock above your head.

For four months, as far as you know, there hasn't been a single human alive on the surface, and without humanity to cull the hordes, the monsters have continuously summoned their own kind, fought amongst each other to evolve, and grown stronger, smarter, and eventually...

*page_break They found their way down.

This room, a reinforced sanctuary magicked into existence via your [Last Stand] spell, was supposed to give you and your two companions time to figure out what to do. Along with its creation, which required every Greater Essence Stone the three of you possessed just to meet the whopping 2,000,000 mana cost, it also created enough food, water, and magically purified air to last ten people for an entire month. 

Beyond the heavy blast doors, a long, narrow hallway stretches toward a distant stairway that once led to the lower districts of Solace, which the [System] had determined would be the most difficult area for the monsters to find. The walls of the corridor are etched with defensive runes that pulse with golden light, ready to repulse any invaders that are unfortunate enough find their way here.

With only the three of you, you should have been able to last for nearly four months in this place. Maybe five, if you rationed.

*page_break Unfortunately, the monsters had other plans...
