The air in bunker city of *Solace* is stale, smelling of cold stone and death. 

When it became clear that the surface was lost, the last standing nations pooled their remaining resources for the Deep-Reach Initiative. Armies of geomancers sheared the earth's mantle to carve these hollows. Architecture mages raised the buildings and created roadways between them. Druids and nature mages wove life into the dark, growing sunless forests and filling reservoirs from deep-earth streams. Illusionists cast spells upon the rock ceiling to simulate a sky and a sun that stayed at high noon, though it faded to a glow like that of the moon during night hours. Finally, the portal mages brought the survivors down, sealing the way behind them. There were no gates or tunnels leading down here; just a mile of solid rock above your head.

For four months, as far as you know, there hasn't been a single human alive on the surface, and without humanity to cull the herds, the monsters have continuously summoned their own kind, fought amongst each other to evolve, and grew stronger, smarter, and eventually...

*page_break They found their way down.

This room, a reinforced sanctuary magicked into existence via your [Last Stand] spell, was supposed to give you and your two companions time to figure out what to do. Along with its creation, which took every Greater Essence Stone you, Matt, and and Callie Owens to pay the whopping 2,000,000 mana cost to cast, it also created enough food, water, and magically purified air to last ten people for an entire month. 
Beyond the heavy blast doors, a long, narrow hallway stretches toward a distant stairway that once led to the lower districts of Solace, where the [System] determined to be the most difficult place to find for the monsters. The walls of the corridor are etched with defensive runes that pulse with golden light, ready to repulse any invaders that are unfortunate enough find their way here.

With only the three of you, you should have been able to last for nearly four months in this place. Maybe five, if you rationed.

*page_break Unfortunately, the monsters had other plans...

“Jesus,” Matt says, running a hand through his hair. “They really did it."

Callie doesn't look up from the machine. She hasn't looked up in three days.

What she's built... what she's *been* building, piece by painstaking piece, for the better part of three months, takes up most of the room's back wall. It looks, generously, like someone welded a grandfather clock to a particle accelerator and then let a fever-dreaming engineer have at it with a blowtorch. Copper conduit spirals around a central housing of dense black ironite-infuded obsidian and intact dungeon cores from Tier 8 and Tier 9 space-time themed dungeons, stripped from every high-tier loot drop the three of you managed to hoard, are slotted into this Frankenstein'monster of a machine. At the center, behind a viewport of reinforced magesteel glass, a single point of light spins.

"Don't worry," Callie says, voice flat as she concentrates on her insane construction, "they'll never find the entrance to this place. Even if they do, all of the abjuration wards at the entrance and through the hallway will stop them. "

Callie finally turns. There are deep, dark rings under her eyes that look like bruises. She's been awake for... you try to count backward... at least fifty hours? Maybe more.

"You both understand what this does," she asks.

You do, and you still can't believe it. 

The machine is an *almost* working, *nearly* calibrated, temporal bridge. Callie Owens, the greatest Artificer alive, possibly the greatest who *ever lived*, has spent the last few months of the apocalypse building a door to the past.

Well, to one specific day in the past: the day the System arrived.
