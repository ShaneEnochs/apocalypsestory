Huge Update
The world ended on a Tuesday.

Not with a bang — or rather, not *only* with a bang. It ended in layers, the way a person dies: first the small things, then the large ones, then the thing that held all the other things together.

The System arrived eight years ago. A notification that appeared in every human mind simultaneously, in every language, in the precise dialect each person would understand best.

*system 
SYSTEM INITIALIZATION COMPLETE. 
Congratulations! Your world has been inducted to the Multiverse.
*end_system

Nobody laughed. Some people cried. Most just stood very still, reading the words that floated in the air before them, and tried to remember how to breathe.

The monsters came three days later.

*title Eight Years Later

*system Essence gained: +5

Eight years of that. Eight years of dungeons and raids and watching people you loved turn into numbers on a screen — their health bars dropping, their names greying out, their entries in the party list blinking once and then going dark forever.

You survived. Somehow, improbably, infuriatingly, you survived.

And now you stand on the ramparts of Castle Voss — the last fortress of the last three humans on Earth — watching the horizon burn.

*system 
Warning: The World Boss [Infernal Demon Lord: Azazel] has been summoned. 
Distance: 8.3 km. ETA: 47 minutes.
*end_system

The notification hangs in your vision like a red wound. You dismiss it. It reappears. You dismiss it again. It comes back a third time, blinking, insistent, as though the System itself is nervous.

*add_item "Last Blade of Democles"

Your hand finds the hilt at your hip without thinking. The Last Blade of Democles — pulled from the deepest dungeon floor on the worst night of your life, still faintly warm to the touch, as if something inside it is still alive. It is the finest weapon on Earth. There are no other candidates for the title.

"Man."

Kai drops down beside you, sitting on the parapet's edge with his legs dangling over a hundred-foot drop like he's on a park bench. He runs his fingers through his hair — longer now than it used to be, shot through with grey he didn't have two years ago. "Just when you think things can't get any worse."

He says it with a laugh. That's the thing about Kai: he has always laughed at the wrong moments. At funerals. In the middle of raids. At the end of the world. It used to annoy you. Now you think it might be the bravest thing you've ever seen a person do.

On your other side, Sable says nothing. She hasn't spoken in three days — not since the Siege of the Eastern Wall, not since she came back alone from a scouting mission that had left with four people. She stands with her arms folded and her eyes fixed on the horizon, and she looks like she is quietly making a decision about something that cannot be undone.

The three of you. The last three. The sky the color of an open wound.
